Common questions

How do I cancel my Pro or Pro Plus subscription?

Subscriptions are managed by Apple, not by us. Open iOS Settings → tap your Apple ID at the top → Subscriptions → Tova Translate → Cancel Subscription. You'll keep access until the end of the current billing period.

You can also tap Manage in the Tova app's Settings screen — it deep-links to the same page.

How do I get a refund?

Refunds for App Store purchases (including Tova subscriptions) are handled by Apple. Submit a refund request at reportaproblem.apple.com — sign in with your Apple ID, find the Tova purchase, and pick a reason. Apple typically responds within 48 hours.

We can't issue refunds directly because we never see the payment. If Apple denies your request and you believe it was in error, email us — we'll write to Apple on your behalf.

I bought Pro but the app says I'm still on Free.

Open the Tova app → Settings → tap Restore Purchases. This re-syncs your entitlements with the App Store.

If that doesn't work:

  • Make sure you're signed into iOS with the same Apple ID you used when purchasing.
  • Force-quit the app (swipe up from the bottom and flick the Tova preview away) and reopen it.
  • If the issue persists, email us with your purchase receipt (Apple sends this to your Apple-ID email) and we'll fix it server-side.
Translation isn't working in mainland China.

Google Translate is blocked by the Great Firewall, so the default engine fails inside China without a VPN. Tova has two fallbacks built in:

  • On-device neural translation (free, all users): Apple Translation on iOS 18+ runs entirely on your device. No VPN, no network. Works the moment you land.
  • Baidu engine (Pro): Baidu's translation API is hosted inside China. Switch to it under Settings → Translation engine → Baidu. Quality is excellent for Chinese↔English and good for ~10 other languages.

If neither works, please email us with what you see — engine availability inside China changes regularly.

How do I download offline translation packs?

Pro users: Settings → Offline packs. Pick the languages you'll need before you fly, tap Download, and the packs install in the background.

On Android, on-device neural translation models (Google ML Kit) are ~30 MB each. On iOS, Apple Translation handles this automatically when you first translate to a supported language on Wi-Fi.

The camera live OCR isn't reading my sign / menu.

A few things to try:

  • Check that the camera is in focus — tap the screen on the text to manually focus.
  • Toggle the scan area with the icon in the top-right of the camera view (3 sizes: tight / normal / wide). Wide helps for whole signs; tight helps for cluttered menus.
  • For handwriting or unusual scripts, tap the shutter button instead of waiting for live detection — the shutter path runs a more aggressive OCR ensemble.
  • For non-Latin scripts (Thai, Arabic, Hebrew, Khmer, Tibetan, etc.), make sure Settings → Camera scan language matches what you're shooting.
The "Listen" button doesn't speak in some languages.

iOS doesn't ship every voice pre-installed — some, like Thai and Vietnamese, are downloadable. If a voice is missing, Tova shows a snackbar telling you which language and where to find it: iOS Settings → Accessibility → Spoken Content → Voices.

Once you download the voice there, restart Tova and the Listen button will work for that language.

My translations are inaccurate.

Tova routes translation through Google, DeepL, OpenAI GPT-4o, or Baidu, depending on your selection. None of these is perfect. For technical or specialised content, try a different engine under Settings → Translation engine — DeepL is often best for European-language pairs, OpenAI handles tone and context well, and Baidu shines for Chinese.

Pro users can also pin preferred translations under Settings → Glossary so a word always renders the way you want it.

Tova is not a substitute for a professional translator for medical, legal, financial, or contractual material.

How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?

Open Tova → Settings → Feedback → Report a bug. The form auto-attaches your iOS version, app version, current language settings, and active engine, which helps us reproduce the issue. You'll get a confirmation email with a reference number.

For feature suggestions, use Settings → Feedback → Feature request or just email us.

Will Tova work on Android?

Yes — an Android version is in active development as of Spring 2026. If you'd like to be notified when it's available on Google Play, email us and we'll add you to the launch list.

Privacy and data collection

Tova collects the minimum data needed to translate text and improve the app. We don't sell your data, and translation requests are not stored beyond the LRU cache used to speed up repeated lookups. Full details, including AdMob (free tier), crash reports, and EU consent flow, are in the Privacy Policy.
